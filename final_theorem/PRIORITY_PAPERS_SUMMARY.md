## PRIORITY 2 (HIGH PAYOFF)

### Paper 2A: Estermann (1930)

**Full Citation**:
```
Theodor Estermann
"On the representations of a number as the sum of two products"
Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society
Series 2, Volume 31, Pages 123–133
Year: 1930
```

**Key Theorems to Extract**:
- Estermann zeta function definition: $E(s; h/k, \alpha) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{\sigma_\alpha(n) e(2\pi i nh/k)}{n^s}$
- Meromorphic continuation to entire complex plane
- Double pole at $s = 1$ (for $\alpha = 0$)
- Functional equation relating $E(s; h/k, \alpha)$ to $E(1-s+\alpha; h'/k, \alpha)$ with modular inversion
- Autocorrelation interpretation for reciprocal-phase sums

**Why Critical**:
- Foundational paper introducing Estermann zeta function
- Essential for understanding the functional equation that relates reciprocal phases to spectral reflection
- Original source for all modern work on autocorrelation machinery

**Retrieval**:
- London Mathematical Society archive (likely free online)
- Project Euclid (mathematical journals archive)
- JSTOR (institutional access)
- Internet Archive (OCR scan may be available)

**Route**: **A (Estermann foundational)**
