## **Immediate Clarifications and Definitions**

### **1. What is a "two-linear-forms Möbius correlation"?**
A **two-linear-forms Möbius correlation** refers to sums of the form:
\[
\sum_{n \leq N} \mu(n) \mu(n + h_1) \mu(n + h_2) \quad \text{or} \quad \sum_{h, k \leq H} w(h, k) \mu(h) \mu(k),
\]
where \( \mu \) is the Möbius function, \( h_1, h_2 \) are linear forms (e.g., \( h_1 = n, h_2 = n + h \)), and \( w(h, k) \) is a weight function. The goal is to bound such sums, often averaged over \( h \) or \( k \).

- **Distinction from Chowla’s conjecture**: Chowla’s conjecture typically concerns single shifts (e.g., \( \sum \mu(n)\mu(n+h) = o(N) \)), while two-linear-forms generalize this to correlations involving two linear arguments (e.g., \( \mu(n)\mu(n+h)\mu(n+k) \)).
- **Uniformity**: "Fixed \( h \)" means bounding for each fixed shift, while \( h \leq H \) means bounding uniformly over all shifts up to \( H \). The latter is harder and often requires averaging.
- **Averaging**: Cesàro (uniform), logarithmic, or other averages are used to smooth out oscillations and make the problem tractable. Logarithmic averaging, for example, often yields stronger results for multiplicative functions.

- **Target bound**: The bound \( C \cdot \sum |w| / \log N \) is a typical goal, where \( w \) are weights and \( C \) is a constant. Variations exist depending on the averaging method and the class of weights.

### **2. Key Papers and Their Roles**

| **Paper** | **Year** | **Core Contribution** | **Relevance to Two-Linear-Forms** | **Technique** |
|-----------|---------|-----------------------|------------------------------------|---------------|
| **Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Tao (MRT)** | 2015 | Averaged Chowla for single shifts | Proves \( \sum_{h \leq H} \left| \sum_{n \leq N} \mu(n)\mu(n+h) \right| = o(HN) \) | Mollification + sieve bounds |
| **Tao–Teräväinen (TT)** | 2016 | Log-averaged Chowla & Elliott | Extends to log-averaging, handles polynomial phases | Nilsequences + entropy |
| **Tao–Teräväinen** | 2019 | Structure of log-averaged correlations | General framework for multiplicative functions, nilsequences, and polynomial phases | Nilsequence decomposition |
| **Harper** | 2021 | Sharp bounds on prime gaps | Improves character-sum bounds (Pólya–Vinogradov type) | Explicit character bounds |
| **Klurman–Mangerel** | Recent | Correlations of multiplicative functions | Character-theoretic approach, conductor bounds | Fourier expansion + divisor lattice |

- **MRT (2015)**: Focuses on **Cesàro-averaged** correlations for single shifts. The mollification technique is central, but extending to two-linear-forms is non-trivial.
- **TT (2016/2019)**: Introduces **log-averaging** and **nilsequences**, which are more flexible for general linear forms. The 2019 paper is the most general and likely the best candidate for two-linear-forms.
- **Harper/Klurman–Mangerel**: Use **character sums** and **conductor bounds** to control correlations. This is a complementary approach, especially for multiplicative functions.

## **Next Steps for Formalization in Mathlib 4**

### **Feasibility Assessment**
1. **Route A1 (MRT)**:
   - **Mathlib Status**: Mathlib has **Dirichlet convolution** and **sieve theory** (e.g., `Mathlib.NumberTheory.Sieve`), but **no Chowla conjecture** or **mollification for Möbius**.
   - **Effort**: High. Requires formalizing mollification, sieve bounds for Möbius, and extending to two-linear-forms.
   - **Key Missing Pieces**:
     - Mollification of \( \mu \) (definition + properties).
     - Averaged Chowla bounds for single shifts.
     - Extension to two-linear-forms (likely requires new theory).

2. **Route A2 (TT)**:
   - **Mathlib Status**: **No nilsequence theory** or **entropy bounds** for multiplicative functions. Log-averaging is also missing.
   - **Effort**: Very High. Nilsequences are a major gap.
   - **Key Missing Pieces**:
     - Nilsequence definition and decomposition.
     - Log-averaging framework.
     - Entropy bounds for correlations.

3. **Route A3 (Harper/Klurman)**:
   - **Mathlib Status**: **Partial support** for character sums (e.g., `Mathlib.NumberTheory.Characters`), but **no explicit bounds** (Pólya–Vinogradov or Harper’s improvements).
   - **Effort**: Medium. Character-sum machinery exists, but explicit bounds and conductor lattice are missing.
   - **Key Missing Pieces**:
     - Explicit character-sum bounds (Harper’s or Klurman–Mangerel’s).
     - Conductor and divisor-lattice machinery.

---

### **Recommended Route for Mathlib 4**
**Route A3 (Harper/Klurman–Mangerel)** is the **most feasible** for the following reasons:
1. **Existing Infrastructure**: Mathlib already has **character theory** (`Mathlib.NumberTheory.Characters`), which is a strong foundation.
2. **Modularity**: Character-sum bounds can be formalized incrementally (e.g., start with Pólya–Vinogradov, then add Harper’s improvements).
3. **Generality**: The character-theoretic approach naturally extends to two-linear-forms via Fourier expansion.
4. **Community Alignment**: Mathlib’s number theory library is actively developed, and character sums are a high-priority area.

**Obstacles to Address**:
- Formalize **explicit character-sum bounds** (Harper’s or Klurman–Mangerel’s).
- Add **conductor** and **divisor-lattice** machinery (if not present).
- Implement **Fourier expansion** for two-linear-forms.
